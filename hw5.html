Manchester United Football Club

History and Achievements

Manchester United Football Club, based in Old Trafford, Greater Manchester, was founded as Newton Heath LYR Football Club in 1878. It changed its name to Manchester United in 1902.

Under the management of Sir Matt Busby, they became the first English team to compete in the European Cup, and won it in 1968. Under the legendary management of Sir Alex Ferguson, Manchester United dominated the Premier League era since its inception in 1992.

Legends like Ryan Giggs, Paul Scholes, and David Beckham became synonymous with the club's success.

The club has a rich legacy of nurturing young talents and promoting them to the first team.

Old Trafford, also known as the "Theatre of Dreams," is their iconic home stadium.

Manchester United has fans spread across all continents, making it one of the most supported football clubs in the world.
